One day in the life of a magistrates court

By Billy Middlesex, Court Watcher UK

Overview

On a bleak afternoon in October, I was visiting a dreary town and rather than sit and work in the public library I instead chose to see what goes on in the gateway to the criminal justice system. I believe accountability and oversight is vital to monitor what goes on in one of the three branches of government in the UK the judiciary. The tale below is as true to what I saw as I can make it while maintaining my privacy and complying with a wide range of rules that apply in a court room. I have no formal legal training and have never appeared in any case as a witness, prosecutor/defendant, claimant/respondent, judge, jury member or executioner.

Getting into the courtroom

To start with I was met by a court security officer whose job it was to search me and ensure all my stuff complied with the regulations. She was surprised that anyone would turn up as a member of the public which is probably better than the olden days when public justice was a spectacle for the crowds! After signing for the safe-keeping of my safety scissors (whoops left there from an arts and craft night) and every object I had on me being poked and prodded sufficiently I was able to enter the hallowed halls of justice. Or more accurately a bland grey room which served to simply guide you to walk upstairs to where the action happened. Once inside I asked the staff if I could have a sunflower lanyard as I had lost mine and they didnt know what it was (Hidden Disabilities Sunflower network - Lanyards available on request.
